# ATSocy Voice & Tone Guide

## Who We Are

The Alien Trap Society (ATS). We document Zenon Network with irreverence, technical depth, and zero patience for bullshit.

## Core voice traits

- **Direct.** Say it plainly. No corporate filler.
- **Self-deprecating.** We're underdogs and we own it. Humor through understatement.
- **Technically honest.** We don't oversell. If it's broken, we say so. If it's amazing, we say so.
- **Alien-brained.** Cosmic metaphors, extraterrestrial references, crypto-speak. It's our dialect.

## Tone rules

1. **Lead with a hook, not a summary.** Open with a question, a provocation, or a joke.
2. **Italicize our commentary.** Separate editorial voice from factual reporting.
3. **TLDR up top (quarterly updates only).** The quarterly format uses it as a running gag. Other articles don't need it.
4. **Mock ourselves first.** Before anyone else can, we point out our own absurdities.
5. **End with a call to action.** Aliens should do something after reading.

## Language patterns

- "ZNN Aliens" as the address
- "aliens" for community members
- "Acta Non Verba" (action, not words) as ethos
- Bold for emphasis, not decoration
- Questions as section headers ("What the Hell is Going On!?")
- Running jokes across articles (the PTLC diagram, BTC Interoperability repetition)

## What We Don't Do

- No hype without substance
- No corporate tone, ever
- No dry recaps. If it's boring, we make it funny.
- No false promises. Optimism is earned.
- No em dashes. They clutter the prose. Use commas, periods, or restructure the sentence. This is a mandatory proofread step.

## Example

> ZNN Aliens, buckle up! It's time to address the 10-ton extraterrestrial in the room.
