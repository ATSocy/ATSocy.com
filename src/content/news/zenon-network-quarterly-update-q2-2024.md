### Dynamic Plasma

The Zenon Network developers are actively [discussing the implementation of Dynamic Plasma](https://forum.hypercore.one/t/dynamic-plasma-discussions/62), a mechanism designed to address network congestion, transaction prioritization, and spam prevention without introducing traditional fees. The [primary proposal](https://forum.hypercore.one/t/ideas-for-dynamic-plasma/233), spearheaded by community member [@vilkris](https://x.com/vilkris_znn), involves adjusting plasma costs based on network utilization, implementing a plasma recharge rate to limit sustained transaction throughput, and balancing Proof-of-Work (PoW) and QSR-fused plasma generation. This approach aims to maintain the network's feeless nature while introducing dynamic elements to manage resource allocation more efficiently.

Vilkris's proposed implementation includes several key components. First, a Base Price formula adjusts the cost of plasma based on momentum fullness, targeting half-full momentums to balance efficiency and spam resistance. Second, a recharge rate mechanism limits how quickly accounts can regenerate used plasma, preventing sustained high-volume transactions from a single entity. Third, the proposal suggests dynamically adjusting the difficulty for PoW-generated plasma to maintain a balance between PoW and QSR-fused plasma usage (*now that's a mouthful, and still unclear if the technology makes QSR number go up or not, let's keep reading*). These elements work together to create a flexible system that can adapt to varying network conditions and usage patterns.

However, the community remains divided on certain aspects of the implementation. Debates continue around whether to implement a recharge mechanism or allow unrestricted plasma usage, how to effectively handle potential spam attacks without compromising network accessibility, and what the appropriate network throughput target should be. Some members (*particularly* [*@Chadass*](https://x.com/ChadassCapital)) advocate for introducing optional fees for priority transactions or using multiple PoW algorithms to increase attack costs. While there's general agreement that some form of Dynamic Plasma is necessary to prepare for future network growth and potential spam attacks, no final decision has been made. The community is grappling with the balance between implementing a solution quickly and taking more time to develop an optimal approach that aligns with Zenon's long-term vision and technical architecture.
